Why visit Porto?

With more than 1.7 million inhabitants in its metropolitan area, Porto is the second largest city in Portugal, after Lisbon. This city, located in the north of Portugal, at the mouth of the Douro River, is a very special place, owning elegant neighborhoods and stately villas that oppose the narrow and ancient alleys that UNESCO has declared a World Heritage Site.

Porto is a city to discover on foot, making the most of its narrow streets full of history and nostalgia. A stroll at dusk along the Douro River, the feeling of entering the Mercado do Bolhão or the taste of its sweet wines are some of the things you can do in Porto. Curiosities

World's Best Destiny

Porto was considered the Best City Destination in the World 2022. The recognition was delivered at the World Travel Awards ceremony in Muscat, Oman.


Eiffel connection

Dom Luis I, Porto's most famous bridge, was built in the 19th century by a student of Gustave Eiffel, the designer of The Eiffel Tower. However, Maria Pia Bridge, which was designed by Eiffel himself in the same century.

Budget friendly for travelers

Porto is very easy on the pocket compared to most european turistic cities. You can get a meal for 5 euros and a hotel for about 20 euros. So if you are visiting Porto, you can enjoy the culture and the scenic beauty without burning a hole in your pocket.

Famous Port wine comes from this region.

This fortified, sweet wine is well known all over the world. It is enjoyed mostly during or after dessert because of its sweet taste. But don’t let the taste fool you, it can have alcohol content as high as 22%. It is made along the Douro river and is one of the chief exports of the region.
