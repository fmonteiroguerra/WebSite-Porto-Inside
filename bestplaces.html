BEST PLACES

Ribeira neighborhood

The Ribeira neighborhood is one of the most important places when it comes to getting to know the historic center of Porto. As its name suggests, it is the area located on the riverbank.

Its colorful and decorated facades attract the attention of all visitors, both those that face the Douro and are reflected in it (best seen from Vila Nova de Gaia, on the opposite bank) and those that are part of the labyrinth of streets that form the neighborhood of Ribeira.

Perfect at night
If you are looking for a beautiful place to have dinner and stroll at night, the Ribeira neighborhood is the right place. You will find dozens of restaurants with terraces, where you can enjoy typical Portuguese dishes while enjoying the view of the Ponte de Luís I and Vila Nova de Gaia with its illuminated cellars.

Lello e Irmão Library

Located in the center of Porto, close to the Clérigos Tower, is what is considered the most beautiful bookstore in Europe.

A neo-Gothic style building built in 1906 houses the Lello e Irmão Bookstore. Inside, several old details manage to create an environment capable of transporting us to a century ago. Its huge wooden shelves full of books reach the ceiling and a beautiful carved wooden staircase reigns in the center of the store, while the stained glass ceiling projects natural light, bringing this unique setting to life.

More than a bookstore, a fairy tale setting
Don't miss a visit to this bookstore which, much more than a place to buy books, is a place to get lost. The magic that surrounds this bookstore is so great that there is an urban legend that J.K. Rowling was inspired by Livraria Lello e Irmão to write the famous Harry Potter saga. If you are a fan of the saga, the Harry Potter free tour of Porto will reveal the relationship between the books and the city.

Clérigos Church and Tower

The Clérigos Church was built between 1735 and 1748 in the Baroque style. It is crowned by the Clérigos Tower, which is one of the city's most emblematic monuments.
This monumental complex from the 18th century was built by the Clérigos Pobres brotherhood in the old center of the city of Porto, in the place known as the “Hill of the Hanged”, as it is the place where the executed were buried.

The Tour
The Clérigos Tower is the tallest tower in Portugal: its 76 meters high and more than 200 steps give access to a privileged view of Porto. On the way up to the tower, you will find 49 bells that form a large concert carillon that will probably give you a good scare if it rings as you pass by. Once you're at the top, all the effort will have been worth it, because you'll be able to enjoy a magnificent view of the city of Porto and take good photos.

Essential
The Church and the Clérigos Tower are some of the most important visits in Porto. It's worth climbing all the steps to get the well-deserved reward of one of the best views of the city.
